                                    PARTIES

1. Plaintiff Hartwell Industries, Inc. ("Hartwell") is a Delaware corporation with its
principal place of business at 445 Park Avenue, New York, NY 10022.

2. Defendant Meridian Capital Group LLC ("Meridian") is a limited liability company
organized under the laws of New York, with its principal place of business at 200 West
57th Street, New York, NY 10019.

3. Defendant David R. Thornton ("Thornton") is an individual who, at all times relevant
to this Complaint, served as the Managing Director of Meridian.

                            JURISDICTION AND VENUE

4. This Court has subject matter jurisdiction pursuant to 28 U.S.C. § 1332 (diversity of
citizenship). The amount in controversy exceeds $75,000, exclusive of interest and costs.

5. Venue is proper in this district pursuant to 28 U.S.C. § 1391(b) because a substantial
part of the events giving rise to Plaintiff's claims occurred in this district.

                                BACKGROUND FACTS

6. On or about January 10, 2023, Hartwell and Meridian entered into a Master Services
Agreement (the "Agreement") pursuant to which Meridian agreed to provide investment
advisory and capital placement services to Hartwell in connection with a $50,000,000
Series B financing round.

7. Under the terms of the Agreement, Meridian was entitled to a success fee of 3.5% of
the total capital raised, payable upon closing of the financing round.

8. Section 7.2 of the Agreement expressly prohibited Meridian from soliciting or accepting
any payment, compensation, or consideration from any third party in connection with the
services provided to Hartwell without Hartwell's prior written consent.

9. Between January 2023 and September 2023, Thornton, acting on behalf of Meridian,
made material misrepresentations to Hartwell regarding the status of investor negotiations,
falsely representing that multiple institutional investors had committed to participate in
the financing round when, in fact, no such commitments had been secured.

10. On September 5, 2023, Hartwell discovered through independent due diligence that
Meridian had secretly accepted a $750,000 "consulting fee" from NorthStar Ventures LLC,
one of the prospective investors Meridian was allegedly soliciting on Hartwell's behalf, in
violation of Section 7.2 of the Agreement.

11. As a direct result of Meridian's breach and Thornton's fraudulent misrepresentations,
Hartwell's Series B financing round failed to close, causing Hartwell to lose a committed
lead investment of $20,000,000 from Blackstone Growth Partners.

                                CAUSES OF ACTION

                              FIRST CAUSE OF ACTION
                              Breach of Contract
                         (Against Defendant Meridian)

12. Plaintiff repeats and realleges the allegations set forth in paragraphs 1 through 11.

13. The Agreement constitutes a valid and binding contract between Hartwell and Meridian.

14. Meridian materially breached the Agreement by: (a) failing to use commercially
reasonable efforts to identify and secure qualified investors; (b) accepting unauthorized
compensation from NorthStar Ventures LLC in violation of Section 7.2; and (c) failing to
disclose material conflicts of interest to Hartwell.

15. As a direct and proximate result of Meridian's breach, Hartwell has suffered damages
in excess of $20,000,000, representing the lost financing commitment and consequential
business losses.

                             SECOND CAUSE OF ACTION
                                    Fraud
                    (Against Defendants Meridian and Thornton)

16. Plaintiff repeats and realleges the allegations set forth in paragraphs 1 through 11.

17. Defendants made material misrepresentations of fact, including false representations
regarding investor commitments, with knowledge of their falsity or with reckless disregard
for the truth.

18. Defendants made these misrepresentations with the intent to induce Hartwell to
continue the Agreement and to refrain from seeking alternative advisory services.

19. Hartwell justifiably relied on Defendants' representations to its detriment.

20. As a result of Defendants' fraud, Hartwell is entitled to compensatory damages of not
less than $20,000,000, plus punitive damages in an amount to be determined at trial.

                            THIRD CAUSE OF ACTION
                             Unjust Enrichment
                         (Against Defendant Meridian)

21. By accepting $750,000 from NorthStar Ventures LLC while ostensibly representing
Hartwell's interests, Meridian was unjustly enriched at Hartwell's expense.

22. Equity and good conscience require that Meridian disgorge this amount to Hartwell.

                                RELIEF REQUESTED

WHEREFORE, Plaintiff Hartwell Industries, Inc. respectfully requests that this Court:

(a) Award compensatory damages in an amount no less than $20,000,000;
(b) Award punitive damages against Defendants Meridian and Thornton;
(c) Order disgorgement of the $750,000 improper payment received by Meridian;
(d) Award pre-judgment and post-judgment interest;
(e) Award attorneys' fees and costs pursuant to the Agreement; and
(f) Grant such other and further relief as the Court deems just and proper.
